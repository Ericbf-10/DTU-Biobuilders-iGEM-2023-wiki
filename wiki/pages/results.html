Validation of the TMS(GFP)

The TMS(GFP) from A. Paul, BBa_K4811002, was tested as the composite part BBa_K4811018 in E. coli BL21(DE3), where the TMS is induced by IPTG. This construct was USER cloned into the high copy number pUC19 backbone for testing. A reporter, consisting of BBa_K4811005 was USER cloned into the low copy number pACYC184 backbone. This has GFP under control of Ptet promoter, and therefore induced by anhydrotetracycline(aTc). It also has mCherry, under control of the pBAD promoter, meaning L-arabinose will induce mCherry transcription. The mCherry transcript has the RBS BBa_K4811000 incorporated, meaning that translation of mCherry should be inhibited by the TMS(GFP).

A schematic of the system to be tested can be seen in Figure 1.

First, the system was tested by induction with varying levels of L-arabinose and IPTG, to find the optimal induction conditions. The results of this can be seen below:

As described in Engineering, 1 mM IPTG and 0.1 % w/v L-ara were found to be sufficient conditions for simultaneous expression of mCherry and the TMS. Using these conditions, GFP was induced by aTc, which showed the following (Figure 2, 3):

The results showed that aTc induced GFP, with 0.5 μM having the highest fluorescence. We would have expected 1 μ$M GFP to have even higher fluorescence, however it seems to be lower, but with a much higher standard deviation associated with the data. With respect to mCherry fluorescence, we were not able to recreate the results of A. Paul, and the only statistical significance was for the measurement of 1 μM of aTc, which had higher fluorescence than the other measurements. However, the corresponding GFP fluorescence was lower for 1 μM aTc than for 0.5 μM aTc. This may be an experimental error associated with the 1 μM aTc measurement. There is also no statistical significance between the 0 μM aTc measurement and the L-ara control, with only L-ara as inducer, and no IPTG.

Validation of new TMS's

Using existing aptamers from the litterature, new TMSs were constructed. This includes two manganese responsive TMSs and two theophylline responsive TMSs. They were characterized by as the composite parts BBa_K4811021(PT7-TMS(Mn1)-TT7), BBa_K4811031 (PT7-TMS(Mn2)-TT7), BBa_K4811022(PT7-TMS(Theo1)-TT7), and BBa_K4811026(PT7-TMS(Theo2)-TT7) in E. coli BL21(DE3), where the TMS is induced by IPTG. The constructs were USER cloned into the high copy number pUC19 backbone for testing. A reporter, consisting of BBa_K4811003 was USER cloned into the low copy number pACYC184 backbone. This has mCherry under control of the pBAD promoter, meaning L-arabinose will induce mCherry transcription. The mCherry transcript has the RBS BBa_K4811000 incorporated, meaning that translation of mCherry should be inhibited by the TMS, if the inhibiting capabilities are conserved upon changing the D-loop.

A general schematic of the system to be tested can be seen in Figure 4

Two theophylline TMSs were tested for mcherry fluorescence based on concentration of theophylline, and we achived the results displayed in Figure 5.

The results showed a statistically significant decrease in mCherry fluorescence based on theophylline concentration for TMS(theo2), but no statistical significance for TMS(theo1), which had a much higher standard deviation associated with its results.

Additionally, two manganese TMSs were tested, and the results can be seen in Figure 6.

Although a decrease in mCherry fluorescence can be seen based on manganese concentration, the results are not as statistically significant as for TMS(Theo2).

Validation of PFOA TMS's

3 different PFOA aptamers were incorporated into the D-loop of the TMS's TMS(PFOA1) - BBa_K4811024, TMS(PFOA2) - BBa_K4811030, and TMS(PFOA3) - BBa_K4811025. These were tested in quadruplicates with varying concentration of PFOA to create the graph displayed in Figure 7.

All PFOA TMSs showed a decrease in mCherry fluorescence based on PFOA concentration. As mentioned in Engineering, this was the opposite as the expected, leading us to revise the model for how the TMS functions with these specific aptamers. Assuming that the observed correlation between PFOA and fluorescence is based upon a change in the structure of the TMS, the model shown in Figure 8 would explain the observed results.
